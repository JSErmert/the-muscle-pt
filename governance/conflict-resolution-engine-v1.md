# THE MUSCLE PT — CONFLICT RESOLUTION ENGINE v1

## Purpose

This document defines how the system resolves conflicts between:
- research and real-world outcomes
- multiple successful interventions
- content performance and mission alignment
- competing signals across cases, content, and system layers

The goal is to ensure that:
- conflicts do not stall execution
- decisions remain consistent
- the system learns from disagreement instead of ignoring it

---

## Core Principle

Conflict is not a problem.

Conflict is signal.

The system must:
- surface conflicts
- evaluate them
- resolve them systematically
- learn from them

---

## 1. OBJECTIVE

The Conflict Resolution Engine exists to:

- prevent contradictory decision-making
- handle disagreement between inputs
- preserve system coherence
- guide resolution without overthinking

---

## 2. CONFLICT TYPES

The system recognizes four primary conflict types:

### 2.1 Research vs Case Conflict
Research suggests one approach  
Real cases produce different outcomes  

---

### 2.2 Case vs Case Conflict
Two similar cases produce different outcomes under similar interventions  

---

### 2.3 Intervention Conflict
Multiple interventions appear to work for the same case type  

---

### 2.4 Content vs Mission Conflict
Content performs well but does not align with mission or positioning  

---

## 3. RESOLUTION HIERARCHY

All conflicts must be resolved using this order:

1. Hard Locks  
2. Decision Preferences  
3. Evidence Strength  
4. Context Specificity  
5. Escalation (if unresolved)

---

## 4. RESOLUTION LOGIC

### Step 1 — Check Hard Locks

If a hard lock is violated:
- the decision is invalid
- resolve immediately by enforcing the lock

---

### Step 2 — Apply Decision Preferences

If multiple valid paths exist:
- choose the option aligned with system preferences
- do not overanalyze beyond preference alignment

---

### Step 3 — Evaluate Evidence Strength

Compare:

- number of supporting cases
- consistency of outcomes
- quality of research
- repeatability

Preference goes to:
- stronger, repeated evidence
- more reliable patterns

---

### Step 4 — Evaluate Context

Ask:

- are the cases truly similar?
- are variables controlled?
- is the population different?
- are conditions comparable?

If context differs:
- both outcomes may be valid
- do not force a single conclusion

---

### Step 5 — Escalate if Needed

If conflict persists:
- move to escalation layer
- mark as unresolved
- gather more data

---

## 5. RESOLUTION OUTCOMES

Every conflict must resolve into one of the following:

### 5.1 Select Dominant Signal
One path clearly stronger

---

### 5.2 Conditional Validity
Different solutions work under different conditions

---

### 5.3 Insufficient Evidence
Not enough data to decide

---

### 5.4 System Revision Required
Conflict exposes flaw in:
- classification
- intervention logic
- system assumptions

---

## 6. DOMAIN-SPECIFIC RESOLUTION

### 6.1 Research vs Case

Rule:
- real-world outcomes take priority if repeated and consistent
- research remains a guiding constraint, not absolute authority

---

### 6.2 Case vs Case

Rule:
- assume context difference first
- do not assume system error immediately

---

### 6.3 Intervention Conflict

Rule:
- multiple interventions can coexist
- prefer the one:
  - with higher repeatability
  - with simpler execution
  - with broader applicability

---

### 6.4 Content vs Mission

Rule:
- mission alignment overrides performance
- high-performing off-mission content must not be scaled

---

## 7. DOCUMENTATION

Every conflict must be recorded with:

- conflict_id
- type
- inputs involved
- resolution outcome
- reasoning
- system version

---

## 8. LEARNING INTEGRATION

Conflict resolution must feed:

- decision preferences (if repeated)
- case engine refinement (if structural issue)
- research mapping (if interpretation issue)

---

## 9. PRIORITY RULE

Conflicts involving:

- repeated failure
- system-level inconsistency
- high-impact decisions

must be prioritized for resolution.

---

## 10. FAILURE MODES

### Ignoring Conflict
System proceeds without resolution

---

### Overriding Evidence Emotionally
Choosing preferred outcome without support

---

### Forcing False Certainty
Declaring a winner without enough data

---

### Over-Resolving
Creating rules from weak or isolated conflicts

---

## 11. SIMPLICITY RULE

Conflict resolution must be:

- fast
- consistent
- evidence-based

Do not:
- overcomplicate decisions
- introduce unnecessary logic layers
- delay execution excessively

---

## 12. FINAL DEFINITION

The Conflict Resolution Engine ensures that:

- contradictions are handled deliberately
- decisions remain coherent
- system integrity is preserved
- learning occurs through disagreement

---

## SYSTEM SUMMARY

Identify conflict  
Check constraints  
Compare evidence  
Evaluate context  
Resolve or escalate  
Feed learning back into system
