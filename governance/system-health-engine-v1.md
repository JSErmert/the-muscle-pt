# THE MUSCLE PT — SYSTEM HEALTH ENGINE v1

## Purpose

This document defines how the system monitors:
- its own usage
- its effectiveness
- its friction points
- its overall performance

The goal is to ensure the system:
- remains usable
- remains efficient
- continues improving
- does not degrade over time

---

## Core Principle

The system must be aware of itself.

A system that cannot evaluate its own health will:
- drift
- become inefficient
- accumulate unnecessary complexity
- lose effectiveness

---

## 1. OBJECTIVE

The System Health Engine exists to:

- detect friction in workflows
- identify underused or overused components
- measure consistency of execution
- surface inefficiencies
- guide system refinement

---

## 2. HEALTH DOMAINS

The system evaluates four health domains:

### 2.1 Execution Health
- are workflows being followed
- is output consistent
- is execution fast and repeatable

---

### 2.2 System Usage Health
- are core documents being used
- are systems being applied in real scenarios
- is the system being ignored or bypassed

---

### 2.3 Decision Health
- are decisions aligned with rules and preferences
- are repeated decisions being eliminated
- is decision-making becoming easier

---

### 2.4 Structural Health
- is the repo clean and navigable
- is structure helping or hindering usage
- is fragmentation occurring

---

## 3. HEALTH INDICATORS

Each domain is evaluated using observable indicators.

---

### Execution Health Indicators

- consistency of posting
- consistency of session structure
- time taken to complete workflows
- frequency of skipped steps

---

### System Usage Indicators

- frequency of case recording
- frequency of outcome reporting
- use of research mapping
- use of prioritization queue

---

### Decision Indicators

- repeated decisions appearing
- adherence to hard locks
- alignment with decision preferences
- reduction in uncertainty over time

---

### Structural Indicators

- ease of locating information
- duplication of files or logic
- number of unused documents
- frequency of restructuring

---

## 4. HEALTH SCORING

Each domain is evaluated using a simple scale:

- 1 = poor
- 2 = weak
- 3 = moderate
- 4 = strong
- 5 = excellent

---

### Overall System Health Score

Average across:
- execution
- usage
- decision
- structure

---

## 5. HEALTH STATES

The system can exist in one of four states:

### Optimal
- workflows are smooth
- system is being used consistently
- low friction
- high output quality

---

### Functional
- system works but has some inefficiencies
- occasional friction
- moderate consistency

---

### Degraded
- system is being bypassed
- frequent friction
- inconsistent usage

---

### Broken
- system is not being used
- decisions are manual again
- structure is ignored

---

## 6. DETECTION RULES

Health issues are detected when:

- workflows are skipped repeatedly
- system components are unused
- decisions are repeatedly re-made
- structure becomes difficult to navigate

---

## 7. RESPONSE ACTIONS

### Optimal
- maintain current system
- observe for future improvements

---

### Functional
- minor adjustments
- refine workflows
- improve clarity

---

### Degraded
- identify friction points
- simplify system
- reinforce core workflows

---

### Broken
- reset to core system
- remove unnecessary layers
- re-establish execution

---

## 8. FEEDBACK INTEGRATION

System health must be reviewed:

- weekly (light review)
- monthly (deeper review)

---

### Weekly Review Questions

- was the system used consistently
- what felt difficult
- what was skipped
- what slowed execution

---

### Monthly Review Questions

- which parts are not being used
- where does friction consistently appear
- what can be simplified
- what should be removed

---

## 9. ESCALATION INTEGRATION

Health issues trigger:

- rule adjustments
- system refinement
- structure simplification

---

### Escalation Rule

If a health issue repeats:
- escalate to system change
- do not ignore recurring friction

---

## 10. FAILURE MODES

### Overbuilding
Too many components added

---

### Underuse
System exists but is not applied

---

### Overchecking
Too much time spent analyzing health

---

### Ignoring Signals
Clear friction but no action taken

---

## 11. SIMPLICITY RULE

System health monitoring must be:

- fast
- lightweight
- practical

Do not:
- create complex dashboards
- delay execution for monitoring
- over-measure

---

## 12. FINAL DEFINITION

The System Health Engine ensures:

- the system remains usable
- inefficiencies are identified
- friction is reduced
- execution stays strong
- the system evolves appropriately

---

Observe usage  
Measure consistency  
Detect friction  
Classify health  
Adjust system  
Maintain performance
