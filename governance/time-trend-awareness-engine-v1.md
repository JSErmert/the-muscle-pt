# THE MUSCLE PT — TIME / TREND AWARENESS ENGINE v1

## Purpose

This document defines how the system:
- tracks changes over time
- identifies emerging trends
- detects fading patterns
- prioritizes recent vs historical signals

The goal is to transform the system from:
- static memory
to:
- time-aware adaptive intelligence

---

## Core Principle

Recency matters.

Frequency matters.

Trends matter more than isolated events.

The system must recognize:
- what is happening now
- what has been happening consistently
- what is no longer relevant

---

## 1. OBJECTIVE

The Time / Trend Awareness Engine exists to:

- detect repeating patterns over time
- identify rising signals
- identify declining signals
- prevent outdated logic from dominating decisions
- improve prioritization accuracy

---

## 2. TIME DIMENSIONS

The system operates across three time horizons:

### Short-Term (Immediate)
- last session
- last few cases
- last few content posts

Purpose:
- detect immediate signals
- guide near-term decisions

---

### Mid-Term (Recent Pattern)
- last 1–4 weeks
- repeated cases
- recurring outcomes

Purpose:
- identify stable patterns
- detect emerging trends

---

### Long-Term (Historical)
- accumulated cases
- established patterns
- validated system knowledge

Purpose:
- preserve system memory
- maintain reliable baseline

---

## 3. SIGNAL TYPES

The system tracks time across:

### 3.1 Case Signals
- recurring case types
- recurring root causes
- intervention success rates

---

### 3.2 Content Signals
- repeated engagement patterns
- recurring audience questions
- high-performing topics

---

### 3.3 Failure Signals
- repeated breakdowns
- recurring errors
- consistent misclassifications

---

### 3.4 Research Signals
- frequently used sources
- repeatedly validated insights
- conflicting or outdated findings

---

## 4. TREND DETECTION

A trend is defined as:

Repeated signal + consistency over time

---

### Trend Categories

#### Emerging Trend
- appearing frequently in short-term
- not yet stable in mid-term

#### Stable Trend
- repeated across mid-term
- consistent outcomes

#### Declining Trend
- previously frequent
- now decreasing in occurrence or effectiveness

#### Noise
- isolated signal
- not repeated

---

## 5. TREND RULES

### Rule 1 — Do Not Overreact to Noise
Single events do not define trends.

---

### Rule 2 — Promote Only Repeated Signals
Trends require repetition across multiple cases or events.

---

### Rule 3 — Recent Signals Override Old Assumptions
When new consistent evidence appears, it must be considered over outdated patterns.

---

### Rule 4 — Preserve Proven Patterns
Validated patterns remain unless contradicted by repeated new evidence.

---

## 6. TREND SCORING

Each signal must be evaluated based on:

- frequency (how often it appears)
- consistency (how stable the outcome is)
- recency (how recent the signal is)

---

### Trend Strength Levels

Low:
- rare or inconsistent

Moderate:
- somewhat repeated

High:
- frequent and consistent

---

## 7. DECAY LOGIC

Signals lose influence over time unless reinforced.

---

### Decay Rule

If a signal:
- has not appeared recently
- is not reinforced by new data

Then:
- its priority decreases
- its influence weakens

---

## 8. PRIORITY INTEGRATION

Time awareness directly influences the queue engine:

- recent repeated signals → higher priority
- older signals → lower priority
- declining trends → deprioritized

---

## 9. CASE ENGINE INTEGRATION

Time awareness affects:

- case classification confidence
- root cause reliability
- intervention selection

---

### Example

If a root cause:
- was previously common
- is now rarely seen

Then:
- reduce its priority
- re-evaluate assumptions

---

## 10. CONTENT SYSTEM INTEGRATION

Time awareness affects:

- topic selection
- content relevance
- audience alignment

---

### Rule

Prioritize:
- current problems
- current audience signals
- current patterns

Deprioritize:
- outdated topics
- overused ideas
- declining engagement themes

---

## 11. FAILURE ANALYSIS INTEGRATION

Repeated recent failures:

- indicate system weakness
- must be escalated quickly

Old isolated failures:

- tracked but lower priority

---

## 12. SYSTEM EVOLUTION

Time awareness drives:

- pattern promotion
- rule creation
- system revision

---

### Evolution Rule

Only promote patterns when:
- repeated
- recent
- consistent

---

## 13. FAILURE MODES

### Static Thinking
Ignoring time and treating all signals equally

---

### Recency Bias
Overvaluing recent events without enough repetition

---

### Historical Lock-In
Over-relying on outdated patterns

---

### Trend Misclassification
Treating noise as trend

---

## 14. SIMPLICITY RULE

Time awareness must remain:

- intuitive
- lightweight
- fast to apply

Do not:
- overcomplicate time tracking
- build unnecessary analytics layers
- delay execution for tracking

---

## 15. FINAL DEFINITION

The Time / Trend Awareness Engine ensures that:

- the system adapts over time
- patterns are recognized accurately
- outdated logic is phased out
- new signals are integrated effectively
