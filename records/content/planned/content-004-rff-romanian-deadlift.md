## The 8-Part Script

### 1. Opening Hook (5 sec)

`[TEXT ON SCREEN — Romanian Deadlift]`

> "On today's episode of Real Functional Fitness, I teach you how to perform the Romanian deadlift."

### 2. Why This Exercise Matters (10–15 sec)

> "Your hamstrings, glutes, and erector spinae are the muscles you use every time you bend over to pick something up off the ground. Every grocery bag, every child, every box. If these muscles are weak — your lower back takes the load. The Romanian deadlift loads them through their longest range under maximum tension. Master this and you protect your back for decades."

### 3. Anatomical Function (10–15 sec)

> "The hamstrings are biarticular — they cross both the knee and the hip. The Romanian deadlift loads them through hip flexion with the knees relatively fixed. The biceps femoris long head, semitendinosus, and semimembranosus all elongate as your hips travel back. The glutes act as the primary hip extensor on the way up. Erector spinae and multifidus isometrically brace the spine throughout."

### 4. Biomechanics and Resistance Profile (10–15 sec)

> "This is a descending resistance profile movement. The mechanical demand on the hamstrings is greatest at the bottom — when the muscle is most lengthened and the moment arm at the hip is greatest. This is the lengthened position. The modest stretch-mediated hypertrophy effect concentrates here."

**Note (2026-05-03):** softened from prior language *"This is exactly where stretch mediated hypertrophy lives"* per research-009 lock at Gate C (refinement-004 first formal Research Authoring Mode execution). Original language was overclaim relative to the evidence (Varovic 2025 PMID 40570881 SMDs 0.05–0.09; Wolf 2025 BFs 0.16–0.3 supporting null). Locked replacement is the operator-confirmed micro-refined two-sentence form.

### 5. Execution and Cues (15–20 sec)

> "Set up with a hip-width stance. Grip the bar slightly outside your legs. Exhale and stack — ribcage over pelvis, neutral spine. Push your hips back as you lower the bar — knees stay relatively fixed. Feel the stretch in your hamstrings. Drive your hips forward to return to standing. Two cues — push the floor away with your hips, not your back. And keep the bar pulled into your body the entire descent."

### 6. Programming (10 sec)

> "Two warm-up sets. 50 percent for 8 reps. 80 percent for 4 reps. Then one working set in the 6 to 12 rep range taken close to failure — 0 to 2 reps in reserve."

### 7. Alternatives (10 sec)

> "If you do not have a barbell or this loads your lower back too aggressively — try the dumbbell Romanian deadlift or the single-leg Romanian deadlift. Both train the same anatomical function with different stability demands."

### 8. Sign Off (5 sec)

> "That is the Romanian deadlift. I am the Muscle PT. I will see you in the next one."

**Total estimated runtime:** 75–90 sec across the 8 sections.

## YouTube Clip Description

```
HOW TO PERFORM THE ROMANIAN DEADLIFT
Real Functional Fitness Exercise Library

WHY THIS EXERCISE MATTERS
Your hamstrings, glutes, and erector spinae are the muscles you recruit every time you bend to pick something up off the ground. They protect the lumbar spine from acting as the primary load path. The Romanian deadlift loads them through their longest range under maximum tension — the position where stretch-mediated hypertrophy is most pronounced.

ANATOMICAL FUNCTION
Primary mover — biceps femoris long head, semitendinosus, semimembranosus (hamstrings — biarticular at hip and knee)
Secondary movers — gluteus maximus (hip extension)
Stabilizers — erector spinae, multifidus (isometric spinal bracing)

BIOMECHANICS
Resistance profile — descending
Peak tension — bottom of the eccentric, hips fully back, hamstrings maximally lengthened
Stretch position — bottom; muscle is loaded longest at end-range hip flexion with knees relatively fixed

EXECUTION
Setup — hip-width stance, bar gripped slightly outside the legs, ribcage stacked over pelvis, neutral spine
Cues — push the floor away with the hips (not the back); keep the bar pulled into the body the entire descent; knees stay relatively fixed
Common mistakes — initiating the descent with knee bend instead of hip hinge; allowing the bar to drift away from the body

PROGRAMMING
Warm-up Set 1 — 50% of working weight for 8 reps
Warm-up Set 2 — 80% of working weight for 4 reps
Working Set — 6 to 12 reps taken close to failure (0–2 RIR)

ALTERNATIVES
1. Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift — same anatomical function with reduced spinal load and increased grip/stability demand
2. Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift — same anatomical function with unilateral stability demand and reduced absolute load

THE RFF METHOD
Train. Stack. Recover.
```
