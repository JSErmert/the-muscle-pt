## Constraint Candidates

- **CC-21.** Specific dosing parameters (frequency, intensity, duration) not established at NMA level — defer to component RCTs and clinical judgment within evidenced ranges (4–8 week windows are typical in the cervical radiculopathy literature)
- **CC-22.** García-Juez authors flag risk of bias and heterogeneity downgrading certainty of evidence in some comparisons. Treat large effect sizes as "robust evidence of meaningful effect" rather than "exact magnitude prediction for any individual case"
- **CC-23.** Long-term outcomes are not the primary focus of research-005's reported abstract findings — short-term pain and disability are the well-evidenced outcomes. Long-term durability requires separate evidence
- **CC-24.** "Cervical radicular pain" in García-Juez includes heterogeneous chronicity (acute, subacute, chronic) across component RCTs — apply with awareness that research-002 specifically addressed chronic, and acute populations may have different prognostic profiles
- **CC-25.** Substituting other manual therapy classes (manipulation, IASTM, dry needling, etc.) for articular + neural mobilization is not evidenced by research-005 — different intervention classes require separate evidence

## Source References

- PMID: 40576779
- Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40576779/
- Title: Effectiveness of Articular and Neural Mobilization for Managing Cervical Radicular Pain: A Systematic Review With Network Meta-Analysis
- Authors: García-Juez S, Navarro-Santana MJ, Valera-Calero JA, Albert-Lucena D, Varas-de-la-Fuente AB, Plaza-Manzano G
- Journal: Journal of Orthopaedic & Sports Physical Therapy, 2025
- Verification: PubMed direct (not Google AI)

Exact figures (from abstract):
- Pain (short-term), articular + neural mob + usual care vs. wait/see/sham/placebo: MD -3.23 (95% CI -4.33, -2.12)
- Pain, articular + neural mob + usual care vs. standard care alone: MD -1.52 (95% CI -2.31, -0.73)
- Disability, neural mob + usual care vs. wait/see/sham/placebo: SMD -1.57 (95% CI -2.53, -0.61) — large effect
- Disability, neural mob + usual care vs. usual care alone: SMD -1.31 (95% CI -1.88, -0.73) — large effect
- 50 studies analyzed quantitatively (777 reports screened)
- Authors note risk of bias and heterogeneity downgrade certainty in some comparisons

### Supplementary (context only — not seed grounding)

- **PMID 40776625** — Núñez de Arenas-Arroyo et al. 2025, Clinical Rehabilitation. Component network meta-analysis, 36 trials, 25 interventions, 8 components. Independent corroboration: **Neurodynamic techniques: SMD = -1.45 (95% CI: -1.88 to -1.02)**. Also: cervical traction SMD -0.66; articular treatment SMD -0.72; dry needling SMD -3.40. The neurodynamic SMD -1.45 from a different NMA aligns with García-Juez's SMD -1.31 to -1.57 — independent confirmation of effect magnitude.
- **PMID 36599029** — Plener et al. 2023, Clinical Journal of Pain. Conservative management systematic review (59 trials, 4108 participants). No specific effect sizes in abstract; "very low certainty evidence" rating across most interventions. Useful as background showing the broader evidence ecosystem.
