## L1 — Source Capture

### Citation

Lutke Schipholt IJ, Coppieters MW, Koop MA, Boellaard R, van de Giessen E, Vleggeert-Lankamp C, Depauw PR, van Berckel BNM, Lammerstma AA, Yaqub M, Scholten-Peeters GGM. Neuroinflammation in the nerve roots and dorsal root ganglion decreases following 6 weeks of neural tissue management: PET/CT imaging findings in a patient with painful cervical radiculopathy. Musculoskeletal Science and Practice. 2026.

PMID: 41620319 | https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41620319/

### Study Design

Case report. PET/CT-based imaging study of neuroinflammation before and after a conservative neural tissue management intervention in chronic cervical radiculopathy.

### Population

N = 1. 56-year-old male. **9-month history of left C7 painful radiculopathy.** Chronic, non-acute, no surgical intervention.

### Variables Measured

- **Volume of distribution (VT)** on PET/CT — quantitative imaging marker of neuroinflammation
- VT measured at two anatomical locations:
  - **Neuroforamen** (site of nerve root exit — the structural compression site)
  - **Spinal cord** (central nervous tissue)
- Pre-intervention vs. post-intervention vs. 6-month follow-up

### Intervention

- **12 sessions** of nerve and joint mobilisation (neural tissue management) over **6 weeks**
- Conservative — no surgery, no injection, no pharmacological intervention specified

### Key Results — exact figures from abstract

- Neuroinflammation at neuroforamen: **VT 12.96 → 6.21** (≈ 52% reduction over 6 weeks)
- Neuroinflammation at spinal cord: **VT 6.43 → 5.38** (≈ 16% reduction over 6 weeks)
- **Reductions maintained at 6-month follow-up**

### Stated Conclusion

Quantitative neuroinflammation in the cervical nerve root region and dorsal root ganglion can decrease following 6 weeks of conservative neural tissue management, with the change persisting at 6 months. This is the first PET/CT demonstration of measurable neuroinflammation reduction with a non-pharmacological, non-surgical intervention in cervical radiculopathy.

## L2 — Insight

### What This Establishes

**Neuroinflammation is measurable and modifiable with conservative care.** Prior to this study, the inflammation component of cervical radiculopathy was largely inferred from biochemical surrogates and treatment response — not directly visualized in vivo. PET/CT-quantified VT puts a number on what previously could only be reasoned about.

The 52% reduction at the neuroforamen is the more clinically interesting figure. The neuroforamen is the anatomical site where the nerve root exits the spinal canal — the structural choke point for inflammation-mediated compression. A 52% reduction in inflammation at that exact site, achieved without surgery or injection, demonstrates that **the inflammation cycle component of chronic radiculopathy is responsive to mechanical-therapeutic input alone.**

### Direct Mapping to the Linked Case

The linked case (query-002, extending query-001) describes a clinically observable version of what Lutke Schipholt measured biochemically:

- **Bump enlargement during flare** = local soft-tissue inflammation visible/palpable at the C-T junction
- **Nerve-like compression and pinching symptoms downstream** = the clinical presentation of elevated neuroinflammation at the neuroforamen
- **Symptoms recede with extension + scapular retraction + inflammation reduction** = the clinical correlate of VT reduction
- **6-week trial framework** = directly applicable as a structured conservative window for the case

The patient profile is also a direct match: chronic (9 months in the study; multi-year in the case), C7-level (cervicothoracic junction — same anatomical region as the case's bump), unilateral (left in study; right in case), painful radiculopathy without red flags.

### What This Does NOT Establish

- **N = 1.** This is a case report, not a controlled trial. Findings are mechanistically plausible and quantitatively documented but not generalizable to populations without supporting cohort/RCT evidence.
- **Direction of causation not proven within the case.** The intervention coincided with VT reduction; without a control arm, regression to the mean and natural history cannot be ruled out at the population level (though the 6-month maintenance argues against pure regression).
- **Does not extend to:** acute traumatic radiculopathy, post-surgical radiculopathy, cancer-related neural compression, cervical myelopathy, or radiculopathy with red-flag features (progressive motor weakness, severe progressive neurological deficit, cauda-equina-equivalent signs).
- **Does not specify which conservative technique within the 12-session protocol drove the change** — nerve mobilisation, joint mobilisation, or the combination. The L2 insight treats the protocol as a unit.

### Pairing with Research-001

Research-001 (Wirth 2014) and research-002 (Lutke Schipholt 2026) sit on adjacent axes for the same case:

- **Research-001:** the *postural-mechanical* axis — thoracic mobility, chest expansion, NDI. Establishes the **structural sequencing** for chronic neck pain cases.
