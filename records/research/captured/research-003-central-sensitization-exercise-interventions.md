# RESEARCH RECORD 003 — Central Sensitization and Exercise Interventions in Chronic Musculoskeletal Pain

This file holds Layers 1 and 2 (source capture + insight). Layer 3 (system mapping) is held at `records/research/mapped/research-003-central-sensitization-exercise-interventions.md`.

---

## L1 — Source Capture

### Citation

Ibrahim AAE, McWilliams DF, Smith SL, Chaplin WJ, Salimian M, Georgopoulos V, Kouraki A, Walsh DA. Comparative effectiveness of various exercise interventions on central sensitisation indices: A systematic review and network meta-analysis. Annals of Physical and Rehabilitation Medicine. 2025.

PMID: 39818121 | https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39818121/

### Study Design

Systematic review and **network meta-analysis** of randomized controlled trials. Synthesizes evidence across multiple exercise intervention types and ranks them on effect size for central sensitization indices in chronic musculoskeletal pain.

### Population

Chronic musculoskeletal pain populations from included RCTs. Specific conditions vary across the included trials (chronic low back pain, chronic neck pain, fibromyalgia, knee osteoarthritis, shoulder pain, etc.) — pooled under the chronic MSK pain + central sensitization umbrella.

### Sample Size

- **249 eligible studies** identified
- **164 RCTs** total
- **89 RCTs** provided data suitable for the network meta-analysis

### Variables Measured

- **Central Sensitization Indices** — primarily Central Sensitization Inventory (CSI) and quantitative sensory testing (QST) measures
- Pre-intervention vs. post-intervention CS index changes
- Exercise intervention category (stretching alone, strengthening alone, aerobic alone, combined modalities, motor control, manual therapy + exercise, etc.)

### Key Results — exact figures from abstract

- **Any exercise intervention vs. baseline:** SMD **-0.81** (95% CI **-0.93 to -0.70**) — large improvement in CS indices
- **Combined stretching + strengthening exercises:** SMD **-1.67** (95% Credible Interval **-2.41 to -0.97**) — very large effect
- **Strengthening + stretching + aerobic exercise (three-modality combined):** SMD **-1.61** (95% CrI **-2.74 to -0.56**) — very large effect

By Cohen's conventions: SMD ≥ 0.8 = large effect. The combined-modality programs sit well above this threshold (1.6–1.7), with the lower bounds of their credible intervals still in large-effect territory.

### Stated Conclusion

Combined exercise programs incorporating multiple modalities (stretching with strengthening, or stretching + strengthening + aerobic) produce substantially larger reductions in central sensitization indices than single-modality interventions or non-exercise controls in chronic musculoskeletal pain populations. All exercise types except *stretching exercise alone* outperformed non-exercise controls.

## L2 — Insight

### What This Establishes

**Combined exercise programs are evidenced at network-meta-analysis level as superior interventions for central sensitization in chronic musculoskeletal pain.** Three independent claims emerge from the figures:

1. **Exercise reduces central sensitization at the population level** (SMD -0.81, large effect)
2. **Single-modality stretching is the only category not shown superior to non-exercise controls** — single-modality stretching alone is insufficient for CS reduction at the population level
3. **Combined modalities produce very large effects** — combined stretching + strengthening (SMD -1.67) and combined stretching + strengthening + aerobic (SMD -1.61) are roughly twice as effective as the average exercise intervention

The clinical translation: when a chronic MSK pain case shows central sensitization features (regional spreading, burning quality, flare/quiescent cycling, elevated CSI scores), the evidenced intervention is **multimodal exercise**, not isolated mobility or stretching work.

### Direct Mapping to the Linked Case

The query-003 linked case has the textbook central-sensitization presentation:

| Case feature | CS substrate |
|---|---|
| Regional symptom spread (right neck → shoulder blade → rear delt → intercostal as one unit) | CS produces widespread pain hypersensitivity beyond original injury site |
| Burning quality during flare | Inflammatory/sensitization quality, distinct from mechanical ache |
| Flare/quiescent cycling | Chronic CS as a recurring rather than constant state |
| Multi-region simultaneous involvement | Spinal cord plasticity / dorsal horn changes propagate across segments |
| Symptoms resolve to baseline between flares | CS modulation is dynamic — sensitization can be reduced, not just chronic |

For this case, Ibrahim 2025 supplies the evidenced intervention class: **combined-modality exercise**, with combined stretching + strengthening or combined stretching + strengthening + aerobic both having very large effect sizes on CS indices.

### Important Sequencing Implication — Refines Research-001 Sequencing Claim

Research-001 prescribed a sequencing bias: "restore thoracic extension mobility before cervical loading; restore chest expansion before strength progression." That bias was framed as mobility/stretching-first. Ibrahim 2025 demonstrates that, for chronic MSK pain populations with central sensitization features, single-modality stretching is insufficient — combined modalities are required.

This does not invalidate research-001. It refines its applicability:

- **Cases without CS features (mechanical-dominant):** research-001 sequencing applies cleanly
- **Cases with CS features (sensitization-dominant or mixed):** research-003 combined-modality framing supersedes — mobility work is one *component* of a combined program, not a standalone first phase

The Wirth + Ibrahim pairing produces a stronger sequencing claim than either alone: assess for CS features first (CSI, QST proxies), then sequence based on which axis is dominant.

### What This Does NOT Establish

- Ibrahim measures CS *indices* (CSI questionnaire, QST measures) — not directly burning quality, regional spreading patterns, or flare frequency. CSI is a validated proxy for these features but not a direct measurement.
- Findings apply to chronic MSK pain. **Acute, post-surgical, post-traumatic acute, and systemic inflammatory disease populations are out of scope.**
- Single-modality stretching alone being shown insufficient for CS reduction does **not** mean stretching is harmful or should be avoided — it means stretching as a *standalone intervention* is insufficient for CS-dominant cases. Stretching as a *component* of a combined program is supported.
- Ibrahim does not specify exact dosing (frequency, duration, intensity) at the network meta-analysis level — those vary across the included RCTs. Practical dosing requires drilling into specific component RCTs.
- Network meta-analyses inherit the methodological quality of their component RCTs. Confidence in the SMD point estimates depends on the risk-of-bias assessment of the underlying trials, which is not detailed in the abstract.

### Confidence Level

**High.** Drivers:
- Network meta-analysis (highest evidence level for comparative effectiveness)
- 89 RCTs in the network (large evidence base)
- Tight confidence intervals on the overall effect (SMD -0.81, CI -0.93 to -0.70)
- Combined-modality SMDs in large-effect territory with lower bounds still above 0.5
- Direct match between the measured construct (CS indices) and the case mechanism (CS features)
- Recent (2025), incorporating contemporary evidence

This is the strongest evidence base of any record authored to date.
