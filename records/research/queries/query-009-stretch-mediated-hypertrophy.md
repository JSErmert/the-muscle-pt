## Core Question

What does the literature establish about hypertrophy outcomes when training emphasizes the **lengthened/stretched position** of a muscle versus the shortened position or full range of motion?

Specifically:

1. **Effect size:** What is the hypertrophy gain difference (% muscle thickness, cross-sectional area, or fiber CSA) between training in the lengthened position vs. shortened position vs. full ROM?
2. **Dose-response:** Is there a dose-response relationship — does longer time-under-tension at long muscle lengths produce greater hypertrophy than shorter durations?
3. **Mechanism plausibility:** What is the proposed mechanism — sarcomerogenesis at long muscle lengths, mechanotransduction under stretch, regional hypertrophy patterning?
4. **Generalizability:** Does the effect hold across muscle groups, training experience levels, and rep ranges?
5. **Practical implication for the lane:** Is the effect size meaningful enough that "this is exactly where stretch mediated hypertrophy lives" (lane spec language) is a defensible claim — or is it overclaim?

---

## Mechanism Focus

- Sarcomerogenesis (addition of sarcomeres in series) at long muscle lengths
- Mechanotransduction — physical stress translated to biochemical signaling for protein synthesis
- Regional hypertrophy patterning along the muscle's length (proximal vs. distal vs. full)
- Length-tension relationship and force production at lengthened positions
- Eccentric loading at long muscle lengths (overlap with eccentric-specific hypertrophy literature)
