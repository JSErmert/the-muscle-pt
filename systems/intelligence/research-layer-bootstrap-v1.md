# THE MUSCLE PT — RESEARCH LAYER BOOTSTRAP v1

## Purpose

Define the exact process for activating the research layer (M2) without disrupting the validated M1 system.

This document ensures:
- research is grounded in real sources  
- mapping is consistent and usable  
- traceability exists without affecting output simplicity  
- the research layer becomes operational, not theoretical  

---

## Core Principle

Build research behind the system before exposing it.

Research informs decisions internally.  
It does not appear in output unless explicitly required.

---

## Objective

Move the research layer from:

defined but empty  

to:

populated, structured, and usable  

---

## Canonical Path

All research must be stored in:

records/research/captured/  
records/research/mapped/  
records/research/archived/  

No alternative paths are allowed.

---

## Research Record Structure

Each research entry must contain:

### Metadata

- research_id  
- title  
- authors  
- year  
- journal  
- PMID (if available)  
- source_link  
- date_extracted  
- extracted_by  

---

### Layer 1 — Source Capture

Direct study extraction only.

Fields:

- study_design  
- population  
- intervention  
- comparison  
- outcomes  
- key_results  

Rules:

- no paraphrasing of data  
- no invented values  
- must reflect original study accurately  

---

### Layer 2 — Insight Extraction

Interpretation of the study.

Fields:

- core_takeaway  
- practical_meaning  
- limitations  
- confidence_level  

Rules:

- no exaggeration  
- no speculation  
- must remain faithful to source  

---

### Layer 3 — System Mapping

Translate research into system behavior.

Fields:

- content_mapping  
- case_engine_mapping  
- decision_mapping  
- rule_candidate  
- constraint_candidate  

Rules:

- must be actionable  
- must be reusable  
- must connect to system logic  

---

## Extraction Process

Follow this sequence for every research entry:

1. Identify Source  
2. Extract Data (Layer 1)  
3. Interpret Insight (Layer 2)  
4. Map to System (Layer 3)  
5. Store Record  

---

## First Activation Rule

Before any scaling:

Create one fully populated research record.

This record must:
- follow the full structure  
- be based on a real study  
- map to a real use case  

---

## Use Case Selection

Start with high-impact cases:

- intercostal strain recovery  
- breathing and rib mechanics  
- posterior chain compensation patterns  

These map directly to system outputs.

---

## Traceability Requirement

Each record must be:

- linked to a real source  
- reproducible  
- timestamped  
- attributable  

No orphaned entries allowed.

---

## Integration Boundary

The research layer must NOT:

- change M1 output behavior  
- introduce citations into standard outputs  
- alter the decision system directly  

It must ONLY:

- support internal grounding  
- prepare for future validation layers  

---

## Failure Conditions

The research layer fails if:

- paths are inconsistent  
- records are incomplete  
- sources are fabricated  
- mapping is unclear  
- no real example exists  

---

## Success Condition

The research layer is successfully activated when:

- canonical paths are used consistently  
- at least one complete record exists  
- mapping is usable by the system  
- traceability is verifiable  

---

## Scaling Rule

After initial validation:

- add one research record per new topic  
- maintain structure consistency  
- avoid bulk population without validation  

---

## Final Definition

This process converts:

external knowledge → structured records → system mapping  

into a usable internal intelligence layer.

---

## System Summary

Define path  
Create one real record  
Validate structure  
Expand gradually  

Build foundation before exposure
